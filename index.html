Case Study

PL Genesis: Frontiers of Collaboration — designing a hackathon that builders actually showed up for.

48h
Hackathon sprint
∞
Web3 builders reached
1
Designer. All of it.

PL Genesis asked me to design the full visual identity and digital presence for their flagship hackathon — Frontiers of Collaboration. The brief was broad but the ambition was specific: create an event brand that felt serious enough for protocol engineers and exciting enough to generate organic buzz across the Web3 builder community.

"The brand had to work at every scale — from a single tweet card to a full event stage. One visual system, zero compromises."

I designed the event identity from the ground up: logo lockup, colour palette, typographic system, and a set of modular social templates that the team could deploy across X without needing a designer for every post.

The website became the campaign's anchor — a conversion-focused landing page that communicated the hackathon's scope, prizes, and community ethos without overwhelming first-time visitors. Every section was designed to reduce friction on the path to registration.

Alongside the digital work, I produced a suite of launch assets: announcement graphics, countdown cards, speaker tiles, and post-event recap visuals — all consistent with the core identity.

Working within the Protocol Labs ecosystem taught me how to design for communities where trust is earned incrementally — through legibility, consistency, and a visual language that respects the intelligence of the audience.
